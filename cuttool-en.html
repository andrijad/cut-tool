How to use CutTool

Enter the profiles you need to cut in the format quantity × width × length (e.g. 4 × 35 × 240). Add each profile on a new line.

Before calculating, check your settings:

  • Coil width — default 125 cm; update to match your actual coil
  • Kerf (cut loss) — material lost on each cut (default 2 mm)
  • Safety margins — clearance on both edges of the coil (default 2 mm each side)

Cutting methods:

  • Strict — each profile length gets its own dedicated pass. Straightforward to execute on any cutting machine; no complex cross-cuts required.
  • Combined — the algorithm attempts to fit shorter profiles into the remaining width of the same pass. Increases efficiency, but requires guillotine cuts running edge-to-edge across the full coil width. Verify your machine supports this before selecting Combined.

CutTool automatically displays the pass layout, efficiency per pass, total scrap and full price breakdown. Click Print Quote to export a PDF with the complete specification.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Coil nesting is the process of arranging cut profiles along the length of a metal coil to minimize material waste. It's fundamentally different from shape nesting software (like ProNest or SigmaNEST), which handles 2D shapes for laser or plasma cutting on flat sheets. CutTool is built specifically for guillotine-style coil cutting — the kind used by roofing contractors, facade installers and metal service centers, where every cut runs edge-to-edge across the coil width. No CAD file required, no enterprise license, no setup.

  • CutTool works from your profile specification. It groups profiles into cut passes, adds kerf loss for each cut and safety margins for both edges, then sums up the total coil length required to complete the job. You see the result broken down by pass, so you know exactly how much coil to order — and where the waste is coming from.

    If you already know your coil dimensions (OD, ID and thickness), the standard formula for coil length is: L = π × (OD² − ID²) / (4 × t)

    CutTool calculates required length from your spec, not from coil dimensions.

  • Strict keeps each group of profiles with the same length in its own dedicated pass. The result is a clean, predictable cut plan that any operator can follow without confusion. Recommended when working with machines that don't have precise stop positioning, or when the cut plan needs to be as simple as possible.

    Combined attempts to use the remaining coil width after placing the primary profile by fitting shorter profiles into that leftover space. Material efficiency goes up — typically 5–15% less waste depending on your spec — but the cut plan is more complex.

    A practical approach: run the calculation with Strict first, then switch to Combined and compare efficiency.

  • Pass efficiency is the percentage of usable coil width that is actually occupied by profiles in a single pass. For example, if your coil is 125 cm wide and the profiles in one pass occupy 108 cm, the efficiency for that pass is 86.4%. The remaining 13.6% is scrap. Overall efficiency shown in the summary is the weighted average across all passes.

What CutTool calculates

Enter your profile list — how many pieces, what width, what length. CutTool arranges them into cut passes using a shelf-packing algorithm that mirrors real guillotine cutting:

  • Accounts for kerf loss on every cut and safety margins on both coil edges
  • Groups profiles into passes using either Strict or Combined method
  • Calculates the total coil length required to complete the job
  • Shows efficiency and scrap per pass and in total
  • Adds material cost and cutting service cost to produce a full price breakdown
